Pythagora's theorem Description


Pythagoras theorem states that for all right-angled triangles.
The square on the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares on the other two sides.
The hypotenuse is the longest side and it's always opposite the right angle.
In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

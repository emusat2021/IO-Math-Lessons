Pythagora's theorem Lesson

Introduction


Pythagoras theorem states for all right-angled triangles.
The square on the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares on the other two sides.
The hypotenuse is the longest side and it's always opposite the right angle.
In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

In the following diagram:

c2 = a2 + b2

Example

Find AC in the diagram below.
AB2 + AC2 = BC2
AC2 = BC2 - AC2
AC2 = 13cm2 - 5cm2
AC2 = 169cm - 25cm = 144cm
AC2 = 144 (122=144)
AC2 = 122
AC = 12

Exercises

  • Exercise 1. Giving the image, if a=16 cm and b=12 cm, what is the hypotenuse's size?


    Correct answer: 20 cm

    Explanation: We have our formula c2 = a2 + b2. We plug in our numbers:

    c2 = 162 + 122

    c2 = 256 + 144

    c2 = 400

    c = 20

  • Exercise 2. Giving the image, if a=16 cm and c=18 cm, what is the b's size?


    Correct answer: 8.24 cm

    Explanation: We have our formula c2 = a2 + b2. We plug in our numbers:

    182 = 162 + b2

    324 = 256 + b2

    68 = b2

    b = 8.24

  • Exercise 3. Giving the image, if b=9 cm and c=14 cm, what is the a's size?

    Correct answer: 10.72 cm

    Explanation: We have our formula c2 = a2 + b2. We plug in our numbers:

    142 = a2 + 92

    196 = a2 + 81

    a2 = 115

    a = 10.72
